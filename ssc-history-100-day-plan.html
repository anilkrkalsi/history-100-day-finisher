SSC History 100 Day Plan

Preparing History for SSC exams often becomes chaotic because the syllabus is large and resources keep expanding. The key to finishing history is not collecting more material, but committing to a fixed completion plan.

This page explains a simple framework to complete SSC History within 100 days using structured daily practice.

Why most SSC History preparation fails

Most aspirants never actually finish history. They keep moving between books, YouTube lectures, and test series without a defined completion target.

  • No fixed number of questions
  • No daily completion metric
  • No measurable progress

Without a defined pool, preparation becomes endless.

The 100 Day Completion Model

A simple way to control SSC History preparation is to use a fixed question pool distributed across a fixed timeline.

  • 100 days total
  • 32 MCQs per day
  • 3200 questions total
  • Balanced distribution across Ancient, Medieval, Modern and Art & Culture

Instead of studying randomly, each day has a defined workload and measurable result.

Daily Practice Structure

A typical day in the plan includes:

  • Attempt 32 MCQs
  • Review explanations
  • Track accuracy
  • Identify weak domains

Over 100 days, this creates complete exposure to a structured history question bank.

Measuring Real Progress

Completion alone is not enough. Progress must be measured using first attempt accuracy.

This is the idea behind the Canonical Accuracy model used in the Syllabus Exhaustion Protocol.

Canonical Accuracy = Correct First Attempts ÷ Total Questions.

Structured Completion vs Endless Practice

Traditional preparation tools encourage endless scrolling and solving random questions. A completion-based system focuses on finishing the syllabus with measurable performance.

The History 100-Day Finisher project is built around this idea: a fixed question pool, a defined timeline, and a clear completion metric.
